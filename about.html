What is Gypsum ?

Chemically known as "calcium sulfate," gypsum contains calcium, sulfur bound to oxygen, and water. Gypsum is an abundant mineral and takes forms including alabaster-a material used in decoration and construction as far back as ancient Egypt. The White Sands National Monument in New Mexico is the world's largest gypsum dune field.

The story of Gypsum Plaster started thousands of years ago. The earliest example of usage of gypsum plasters is at the Tomb of Tutankhamen at the Giza Pyramids which was internally lined with gypsum plasters. Today Global Gypsum range of gypsum plaster has replaced sand and cement plaster as the preferred choice for all internal plastering with all leading builders and contractors across India.
